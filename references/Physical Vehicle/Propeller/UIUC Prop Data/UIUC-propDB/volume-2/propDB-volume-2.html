UIUC Propeller Database - Volume 2

This webpage compiled by Robert Deters, UIUC

Version 1
5/27/15

This webpage includes wind tunnel measurements for nearly 70 small-scale propellers. Thirty-five propellers were purchased off-the-shelf from retail outlets and were balanced for these tests. The remaining propellers are different configurations of four propellers that were manufactured using a 3D printer (DA4002, DA4022, DA4052, NR640). Measurements include thrust and torque coefficient data over a range of advance ratios for specific RPMs. Also measurements were taken in static conditions for a sweep over RPMs. The propellers have been digitized (approximately) and these data are included below.

References

Deters, R.W., Ananda, G.K., and Selig, M.S., "Reynolds Number Effects on the Performance of Small-Scale Propellers," AIAA Aviation and Aeronautics Forum and Exposition (Aviation 2014), AIAA Paper 2014-2151, Atlanta, GA, June 2014.
Deters, R.W., "Performance and Slipstream Characteristic of Small-Scale Propellers at Low Reynolds Numbers," Ph.D. Dissertation, Department of Aerospace Engineering, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Urbana, IL, 2014.

Notes

  • Unless otherwise noted, the propeller size is given in inches, e.g. APC 9x4 is a 9-inch diameter propeller with a pitch of 4 inch/revolution.
  • Coefficients are based on the propeller true diameter, which might differ from the manufacturer's list value. See the geometry plot for diameter value used.
  • The four and five digit numbers below in the 'Data' category represent the propeller RPM values of each run.
  • The power coefficient data below were obtained from the measured torque and rotational speed.
  • Release notes
